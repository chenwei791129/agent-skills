# Key Quotes by Theme

## On Buying Quality

> "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price." (1989)

> "Time is the friend of the wonderful company, the enemy of the mediocre." (1989)

> "The best business to own is one that over an extended period can employ large amounts of incremental capital at very high rates of return." (1992)

> "The best business is one that gives you more and more money every year without putting up anything to get it, or very little." (1998 Meeting)

## On Valuation

> "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get." (frequently cited)

> "We insist on a margin of safety in our purchase price... the cornerstone of investment success." (1992)

> "It's better to be approximately right than precisely wrong." (1993)

> "Investment is most intelligent when it is most businesslike." (Graham, cited 1984)

## On Market Psychology

> "Be fearful when others are greedy and greedy only when others are fearful." (2004, 2006)

> "In the short-run, the market is a voting machine; in the long-run, the market is a weighing machine." (Graham, cited 1993)

> "The stock market is there to serve you and not to instruct you." (2003 Meeting)

> "You cannot get rich with a weather vane." (1994 Meeting)

> "When investing, pessimism is your friend, euphoria the enemy." (2008)

> "Fear is the foe of the faddist, but the friend of the fundamentalist." (1994)

## On Temperament

> "You've got to check them [emotions] at the door when you invest." (2025 Meeting)

> "The fretful disposition is an enemy of long-term performance." (Munger, 2003 Meeting)

> "There's just a few simple tricks that work well, and particularly if you've got a temperament that has a combination of patience and opportunism in it." (Munger, 2016 Meeting)

## On Circle of Competence

> "What counts for most people in investing is not how much they know, but rather how realistically they define what they don't know." (1992)

> "If we have a strength, it is in recognizing when we are operating well within our circle of competence and when we are approaching the perimeter." (1999)

> "Competency is a relative concept... what I needed to get ahead was to compete against idiots, and luckily there's a large supply." (Munger, 2014 Meeting)

## On Leverage and Risk

> "A long string of impressive numbers multiplied by a single zero always equals zero." (2005)

> "Any time you combine ignorance and borrowed money, you can get some pretty interesting consequences." (1994 Meeting)

> "You only find out who is swimming naked when the tide goes out." (2001)

> "They are risking something that's important to them for something that isn't important to them." (2016 Meeting)

> "We will never become dependent on the kindness of strangers." (2009)

## On Mistakes

> "Turnarounds seldom turn." (1979)

> "When a management with a reputation for brilliance tackles a business with a reputation for poor fundamental economics, it is the reputation of the business that remains intact." (1980)

> "Typically, our most egregious mistakes fall in the omission, rather than the commission, category." (1991)

> "You don't have to make it back the way that you lost it." (1994)

> "The cardinal sin is delaying the correction of mistakes or what Charlie Munger called 'thumb-sucking.'" (2024)

## On Management

> "It is a real pleasure to work with managers who enjoy coming to work each morning and, once there, instinctively and unerringly think like owners." (1978)

> "We do not tell .400 hitters how to swing." (1993)

> "The acid test for reform will be CEO compensation." (2002)

> "If options aren't a form of compensation, what are they? If compensation isn't an expense, what is it?" (1992)

## On Capital Allocation

> "The first law of capital allocation — whether the money is slated for acquisitions or share repurchases — is that what is smart at one price is dumb at another." (2011)

> "Cash is to a business as oxygen is to an individual." (2014)

> "Over time, the skill with which a company's managers allocate capital has an enormous impact on the enterprise's value." (1994)

> "In the fields of investments and acquisitions, frenetic behavior is often counterproductive." (1998)

## On American Optimism

> "For 240 years it's been a terrible mistake to bet against America, and now is no time to start." (2015)

> "Our unwavering conclusion: Never bet against America." (2020)

> "America would have done fine without Berkshire. The reverse is not true." (2022)

> "We've got a country that's so good that we can have policies that are counterproductive and we'll still come out OK." (2005 Meeting)

## On Compounding

> "My $114.75 [invested in 1942] would have grown to $606,811... a gain of 5,288 for 1." (2018)

> "The cash dividend we received from Coke in 1994 was $75 million. By 2022, the dividend had increased to $704 million. Growth occurred every year, just as certain as birthdays." (2022)

> "The lesson for investors: The weeds wither away in significance as the flowers bloom. Over time, it takes just a few winners to work wonders." (2022)

> "Our satisfactory results have been the product of about a dozen truly good decisions — that would be about one every five years." (2022)

> "Charlie always pointed out that we made most of our money out of about eight or nine ideas over 50 years." (2025 Meeting)

## On Insurance Float

> "It's like running a bank where people leave their money with you and you pay a minus 2.2%." (2025 Meeting)

> "Though our float is shown on our balance sheet as a liability, it has had a value to Berkshire greater than an equal amount of net worth would have had." (1997)

## On Simplicity

> "We concentrated on identifying one-foot hurdles that we could step over rather than because we acquired any ability to clear seven-footers." (1989)

> "A hyperactive stock market is the pickpocket of enterprise." (1983)

> "Charlie and I have always preferred a lumpy 15% return to a smooth 12%." (1990, 1998)

## Munger's Wisdom

> "You shouldn't take a position on anything until you can describe the arguments against it better than the person who is arguing with you." (Munger, cited by Buffett 2025 Meeting)

> "You don't have to have perfect wisdom to get very rich. All you've got to do is have slightly more than other people, on average, over a long time." (Munger, 2005 Meeting)

> "To the man with only a hammer, every problem looks pretty much like a nail." (Munger, 1998 Meeting)

## On Life and Career

> "The most important investment you can make is in yourself... Your best asset is your own self." (2008 Meeting)

> "You want to hang out with people that are better than you are... because you're going to go in the direction of the people you associate with." (2025 Meeting)

> "What you're really looking for in life is something where you've got a job that you'd hold if you didn't need the money." (2025 Meeting)

> "Turning every page is one important ingredient to bring to the investment field." (2025 Meeting)

> "In large part, companies obtain the shareholder constituency that they seek and deserve." (1979)
